GRBL LIMIT SWITCH SETUP — HOMING CONFIGURATION
================================================

AXIS CONVENTION
---------------
- Positive X/Y = working/jogging direction (home is at the min corner)
- Positive Z = up, Negative Z = down (home is at the top of Z travel)


STEP 1: WIRE THE LIMIT SWITCHES
--------------------------------
Use normally-open (NO) microswitches.
For each switch, connect:
  - One terminal  →  signal pin on CNC shield (X-, Y-, or Z-)
  - Other terminal →  GND pin on CNC shield

Switch placement:
  - X switch: at the MINIMUM X end (the corner the gantry starts from)
  - Y switch: at the MINIMUM Y end (the corner the gantry starts from)
  - Z switch: at the TOP of Z travel (maximum Z position)


STEP 2: OPEN A SERIAL TERMINAL TO THE ARDUINO
----------------------------------------------
Baud rate: 115200
(Use Arduino Serial Monitor, PuTTY, or similar)


STEP 3: SEND THESE GRBL SETTINGS
----------------------------------
Type each line and press Enter, wait for "ok" before the next:

  $22=1    <- enable homing cycle ($H command)
  $23=3    <- invert X and Y homing direction so they home toward -X/-Y (min switches)
             Z is NOT inverted — GRBL homes Z upward (+Z) by default, which is what we want
  $21=1    <- enable hard limits (machine stops if a switch is hit during normal operation)

To verify settings were saved, send:  $$
Look for $22, $23, $21 in the output.


STEP 4: TEST HOMING
--------------------
Send:  $H

Expected sequence:
  1. Z moves UP first until it hits the top switch
  2. X and Y move toward their min switches simultaneously
  3. Machine reports "ok" when done

After homing, send:  ?
Position should show:  WPos:0.000,0.000,0.000


STEP 5: VERIFY JOGGING DIRECTION
----------------------------------
Jog in +X → machine should move AWAY from the X switch
Jog in +Y → machine should move AWAY from the Y switch
Jog in -Z → welding head should move DOWN toward the workpiece


TROUBLESHOOTING
---------------
- If Z moves DOWN instead of up during homing: add 4 to $23 (set $23=7)
- If X or Y homes in the wrong direction: remove 1 or 2 from $23 accordingly
- If homing triggers immediately without moving: check switch wiring (may be shorted)
- If homing never triggers: check switch wiring (may be open / wrong pins)
- "ALARM:1" after $H = limit switch hit before homing started, send $X to unlock then retry
